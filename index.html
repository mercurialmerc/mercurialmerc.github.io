Template to communicate Research Insights to Policymakers (TRIP)

This template is designed to bridge the gap between technical AI research and policy understanding. Each section is structured to convey information in a way that's accessible to policymakers while preserving the technical accuracy of the research. Moving down the sections should provide increasing context. (View the about page for additional context)

THE GIST

This section aims to provide a brief top-level overview of the papers' main points.

So... What? (Policy Bottom line)

[A one or two liner summarizing the paper's essence. Why should policymakers care? This is the elevator pitch.]

Governance considerations

[How might this affect AI governance? What are potential policy actions?]

THE WAVE

This section provides a more in-depth summary of the paper, consisting of a summary of the main points followed by a brief reflection on stakeholders who may have a stake in the topics discussed.

Key Context and Findings

[What does the paper describe, and what are its results? Can include background problem/motivation, comparison to other methods/context, approach/methods, main findings, limitations, dual use concerns, conclusions, and future work. Important technical terms are defined.]

Stakeholders

[Who should care? Which industries are affected?]
